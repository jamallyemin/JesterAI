Privacy Policy

EFFECTIVE DATE: April 25, 2026  |  EXTENSION: Jester AI  |  VERSION: 1.1

Overview

Jester AI is a Chrome browser extension that uses AI to detect phishing and brand-impersonation threats in real time. This policy explains what data the extension accesses, how it is used, and what is never collected.

✓ Jester AI does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to its own servers. The developer has no backend and receives no user data.

Data the Extension Accesses

To perform phishing analysis, Jester AI accesses the following on each page you visit:

  • Page URL and hostname — used to identify suspicious domain patterns.
  • Page text content — scanned locally to detect brand name mentions (e.g. "Google", "PayPal").
  • Form structure — checks whether a page contains login or password input fields, which increases phishing risk.
  • Page headings and button labels — sent to the Gemini AI API as metadata for threat analysis.
  • Page screenshot — captured and sent to the Gemini API for visual brand-impersonation analysis (deep scan mode only).
None of this data is sent to Jester AI's developer. It is sent directly from your browser to the Google Gemini API using your own API key.

Your Gemini API Key

To use Jester AI, you must provide a Google Gemini API key. This key is:

  • Stored locally in your browser using chrome.storage.local — it never leaves your device except to make requests to Google's API.
  • Encoded with Base64 in storage. Note: this is obfuscation, not encryption. Your key remains on your device only.
  • Never transmitted to the extension developer or any third party other than Google.

You can remove your API key at any time via the extension settings panel (⋮ icon).

Third-Party Services

Jester AI communicates with two external services:

  • Google Gemini API (generativelanguage.googleapis.com) — page metadata and screenshots are sent here for AI threat analysis. Google's privacy policy applies: policies.google.com/privacy
  • OpenPhish Public Feed (raw.githubusercontent.com) — a publicly available list of known phishing URLs, fetched every 12 hours and stored locally in your browser for fast matching.

No other external connections are made by this extension.

Local Storage

The following data is stored in chrome.storage.local (on your device only):

  • Your Gemini API key (Base64-encoded)
  • Your whitelist of trusted domains
  • The last scan verdict (status, reason, rating) for the current tab
  • The cached OpenPhish URL list
  • Your on/off preference for the extension

All of this data stays on your device. You can clear it by removing the extension or via Chrome's extension storage settings.

What We Do NOT Collect

  • Your name, email address, or any account information
  • Your browsing history
  • Passwords or form field values
  • Any analytics, crash reports, or usage data
  • Any data sent to a developer-controlled server
✓ The developer of Jester AI has no server, no database, and no access to any user data.

Permissions Used

  • activeTab — to read the current page's URL and content for scanning.
  • storage — to save your settings and whitelist locally.
  • tabs — to identify which tab triggered a scan and reload tabs when needed.
  • notifications — to alert you when a high-risk phishing site is detected.
  • <all_urls> — required to scan any website you visit. No browsing data is collected from this permission.

Children's Privacy

Jester AI is not directed at children under the age of 13 and does not knowingly collect any data from them.
